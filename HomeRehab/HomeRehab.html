Home Rehab

Designer 2014-2015

Home Rehabilitation System





Background

Stroke is a leading cause of long time disability in United States. Majority survivors need regular rehabilitation to gain recovery. But therapy resources, cost and time are limited. Our system uses distributed cognition design to address the problem and leverages the full network of interactions between environment, tools, artifacts and people.





Our Idea

We came up with a series of objects which match the WMFT and FEIX. These objects are connecting standard clinical assessment (Wolf Motor Function Test) and computer version assessment (Feix Grasp Taxonomy). Translating traditional daily things into abstract and modular object.



Mapping the standard clinical assessment (Wolf Motor Function Test) with and computer version assessment (Feix Grasp Taxonomy)





Object Design



In order to make objects more useful, we adapted modular design principle into our design. Every object could encourage several hand grasps, at the same time, they are also being considered as modular parts to combine with other objects. Modular parts could make object more adaptive, reduce the number of objects and add possibilities of grasps. Since every stroke survivor has his/her own difficulty. The modular part can be chosen and combined based on different patients’ requirement. The difficulty level of grasping could be adjusted. They can move and lift objects by changing the modular parts.
